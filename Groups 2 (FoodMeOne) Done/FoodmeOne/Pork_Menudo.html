The Pork Menudo recipe that I am featuring today is the Filipino version. It is a type of pork stew cooked in a tomato based sauce. The basic way of cooking this dish involves fatty cuts of pork such as shoulder, butt, or belly. It also makes use of pig liver and vegetables such as carrot and potato.

I have shown you how to cook different versions of menudo in this food blog throughout the years. There were festive versions, and there are variations that involve the use of meats other than pork. Feel free to browse around to see these recipes.

This pork menudo recipe is my favorite. I use it for both everyday meals and special occasions. I think you'll agree once you try it. Pinoy menudo is different from Mexican menudo. Let me explain the differences.

Filipino Pork Menudo Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork cubed
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube
  • ¼ lb pig liver cubed
  • 1 piece potato sliced
  • 1 piece carrot cubed
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • 3 pieces calamansi
  • 1 piece onion minced
  • 3 cloves garlic minced
  • ½ teaspoon ginger minced
  • ½ teaspoon granulated white sugar
  • 1 can tomato sauce (8 oz)
  • 1½ cups water
  • 3 pieces bay leaves sliced diagonally
  • 4 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil

Cooking Instructions

  1. Heat 1½ tablespoons oil in a pot. Saute ginger. Add liver and continue to saute until light brown. Remove from the pot. Set aside.
  2. Pour remaining oil into the pot. Saute onion and garlic.
  3. Add marinated pork once the onion softens. Cook until light brown.
  4. Pour remaining marinade, tomato sauce, and water. Let boil.
  5. Add Knorr pork cube. Stir.
  6. Cover the pot and cook until pork becomes tender. Note: use between low to medium heat. Add more water as needed.
  7. Put the sliced liver back in the pot and then add potato and carrot. Cover and continue to cook in medium heat for 8 to 10 minutes.
  8. Season with sugar, salt, and ground black pepper. Cook for 2 more minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve and enjoy!
